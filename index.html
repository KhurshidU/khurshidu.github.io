§ 01 About

Chasing hidden particles between Vancouver and CERN.

I am a master’s student in physics at the University of British Columbia and TRIUMF, Canada’s particle accelerator centre. On the ATLAS experiment at CERN I am part of the Long-Lived and Unconventional Physics Searches group, working on a search for long-lived particles that decay into pairs of muons - a signature that could reveal dark photons and other hidden-sector physics. My day-to-day centres on displaced-muon and primary-vertex reconstruction, using both detector simulation and LHC data.

I earned my BSc in Honours Mathematical Physics with Co-op at the University of Waterloo, alongside minors in Astrophysics and Pure Mathematics. My path through physics has been unusually broad, spanning work on dark matter experiments two kilometres underground at SNOLAB, quantum control research at the Institute for Quantum Computing, and jet reconstruction studies for a future Muon Collider.

Originally from Tashkent, Uzbekistan, I am an active science communicator, giving public talks on particle physics and dark matter - from the H.R. MacMillan Space Centre and Nerd Nite in Vancouver to community audiences back home.

§ 03 Research

From colliders to deep-underground detectors.

My work sits at the experimental edge of particle physics and the search for dark matter - looking for the faint, unconventional signatures that the Standard Model leaves unexplained.

ATLAS · displaced muons

Long-lived particles & dark photons

Searching for particles that travel a measurable distance before decaying into muon pairs - a window onto dark photons and hidden sectors. I work on displaced-muon and primary-vertex reconstruction with the ATLAS detector.

Muon Collider · TRIUMF

Future colliders

Jet-reconstruction and new-physics studies for a proposed Muon Collider, including techniques to suppress muon-decay backgrounds and probe the Higgs self-interaction - the basis of my undergraduate thesis.

SNOLAB · DarkSide-20k / DEAP

Dark-matter direct detection

Monte Carlo simulations and signal studies for argon and xenon rare-event experiments - DarkSide-20k, DEAP-3600, and nEXO/LoLX - including detector work two kilometres underground at SNOLAB.

IQC · open quantum systems

Quantum information

Control of open quantum systems using Average Hamiltonian Theory at the Institute for Quantum Computing, with simulations carried out in Mathematica.
